Aaron Judge's History, From Birth to Now

Early Life

Judge was born April 26, 1992 in Linden, California where he was adopted by Patty and Wayne Judge who were teachers. He grew up a San Francisco Giants fan and played multiple sports in high school. He played football, baseball, and basketball where he stood out in all the sports, setting records and receiving accolades in all of them. He was recruited to play Division 1 football at schools like Notre Dame, Stanford, and USC, but decided to stick with baseball, playing at Fresno State.

College Career

He was selected by the Oakland Atheltics in the 2010 MLB Draft but decided to stay in college, where he played for Fresno State in the Western Athletic Conference where he won the WAC Championship, qualified for the Division 1 Baseball Tournament, and was named a Freshman All-American. The year after, he won the 2012 TD Ameritrade College Home Run Derby. Judge lead the team in homeruns, and runs batted in in his junior year, and was drafted by the New York Yankees in 2013 with the 32nd Overall Pick.

Minor League Career

Throughout his 4 year career in the Minors, Judge dominated at all levels. In Single A, Double A, and Triple A, Judge was in the top 10 batters in almost all metrics of batting, leading to get promoted almost every year, until he eventually made it to the major leagues in 2016. In 2015 and 2016, he was selected as a Future All-Star, and he was invited to the Yankees Major League Spring Training to show his talent playing against other major leaguers. He made his official MLB debut shortly after.

Major League Career

Aaron made his MLB Debut on August 13, 2016 where he homered on his first at bat, and homered in his second start as well. He had a decent 2016, but in 2017, his Rookie Season, he started almost all the games and had a great season. He won Rookie of the Month 4 months in his rookie season and dominated, breaking numerous Yankees records, and continuing to do so today. He won the Home Run Derby his rookie season as well, leading to the MLB Commissioner saying he could become the face of baseball. Aaron Judge is currently on pace to break the Single Season Home Run Record, and has been leading the MLB in almost all batting statistics, with the posibility of winning a Triple Crown, meaning he leads in Batting Average, Homeruns, and Runs Batted In. He is entering Free Agency this offseason, meaning his new contract will also break records.
